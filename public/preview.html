// THE METHOD

Two zodiacs, one sky.

Every natal chart begins with a choice most software never mentions: where does the zodiac start? The tropical answer is the March equinox — the Sun crossing the celestial equator, the year's own geometry. The sidereal answer is the stars themselves, the zodiac as the sky actually stands. Around 285 CE the two answers coincided. They have been drifting apart ever since, and the offset now stands at 24°13′. Most astrology quietly picks one answer and discards the other. AXIS computes both, because a person is not obliged to live in only one sky.

// PRECESSION

The offset is not an error.

The Earth's axis traces a slow circle against the stars — one revolution in roughly twenty-six thousand years — so the equinox creeps backward through the constellations at 50.2564″ a year. The two zodiacs are both correct; they are simply measuring from different anchors, and the offset between the anchors grows. That offset is the ayanamsa. AXIS uses Lahiri — 23°51′ at the J2000 epoch, within a hundredth of a degree of the IAU reference frame — carried forward to the minute of birth, and displayed on every chart it casts. Nothing about the offset is hidden, rounded, or averaged. The offset is the point.

// CONVENTION CALLS

Where AXIS departs from habit.

TRUE NODE OVER MEAN

The mean node is a long-term average; the true node is where the lunar nodal axis actually stood at birth. It oscillates about ±1.5° around the mean on a roughly 173-day period — enough to move Rahu into a different nakshatra. Standard Jyotish convention takes the average. AXIS takes the sky.

MC ≠ 10TH CUSP

Under Whole Sign houses the tenth house begins where the tenth sign begins. The Midheaven is a different fact entirely — the ecliptic's crossing of the local meridian — so AXIS draws the MC as its own angle rather than collapsing the two into one line.

WHOLE SIGN × 2

Both charts use Whole Sign houses, applied identically to planets, wheel, and interpretation — so when a house shifts between systems, it is because the sky moved, never because the method changed under you.

VIMSHOTTARI, CONTEXT ONLY

The dasha sequence appears as the chapter a life is currently reading — a lens on emphasis, not a schedule of events. AXIS does not predict, and a timing system is not an excuse to start.

// THE VOICE

What a reading will never do.

No predictions — the chart is structure, not schedule. No prescriptions, no affirmations, no mystical padding, no wellness-industry warmth. The house rule is precision over poetry: when a sentence could be analytically sharp or lyrically evocative, AXIS chooses sharp, because the observation that makes you think that is exactly right is worth more than the beautiful one that produces a vague feeling.

Depth comes from specificity, not length. Every sentence earns its place — on the bright side of a person exactly as much as on the shadow side. A reading exists to show a whole person, not to diagnose what is wrong with one; struggle is one colour in the portrait, never the whole of it. Precision is the warmth.
